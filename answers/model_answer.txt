Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae, and some bacteria use sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water to synthesise food in the form of glucose and release oxygen as a by-product. This process takes place in the chloroplasts, which contain the green pigment chlorophyll. The overall chemical equation is: 6CO2 + 6H2O + light energy → C6H12O6 + 6O2. Photosynthesis is essential for life on Earth as it forms the base of most food chains and is responsible for the oxygen in the atmosphere.
